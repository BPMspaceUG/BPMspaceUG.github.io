Real-World Scenarios

How AI on Boat Helps You

Storm Avoidance

Scenario: You're planning a weekend cruise. The AI analyzes weather patterns along your route and alerts you: "Storm system approaching your destination in 18 hours with 35kt winds. Recommend delaying departure or changing course to alternate anchorage."

Engine Overheating Prevention

Scenario: While underway, coolant temperature rises above normal. The AI immediately alerts you: "Engine temp 15°F above baseline. Possible impeller failure or cooling system blockage. Reduce RPM and check raw water intake." Preventing costly engine damage.

Smart Fuel Planning

Scenario: Planning a long passage, the AI calculates: "Current fuel: 65%. Distance to destination: 120nm. Estimated consumption: 72%. Nearest fuel dock at Marina Bay (30nm off course) has diesel at $3.89/gal. Recommend refueling before departure."

Perfect Anchorage Finder

Scenario: Evening approaches and you need to anchor. The AI suggests: "Recommended: Hidden Cove (2.3nm NE). Protection from SW winds, 12-15ft depth, good holding in mud. Nearby: Waterfront Grill (4.5★, fresh seafood). No anchoring restrictions."

Permit Compliance

Scenario: Crossing into new waters, the AI reminds you: "Entering County Water District. Pump-out permit required ($45, valid 30 days). Your current permit expires in 3 days. Nearest purchase location: Harbormaster Office, Pier 12."

Battery Management

Scenario: At anchor overnight, the AI monitors power: "House battery at 68% (11.9V). Current consumption: 4.2A. Projected runtime: 14 hours. Refrigerator and navigation lights consuming 85% of power. Consider starting generator or reducing load."

Open Source Platform

What's Included (MVP)

Data Bus & Integrations

  • NMEA 2000/0183 via CAN bus
  • Signal K server (open marine data standard)
  • MQTT messaging for real-time updates
  • RESTful API for third-party integrations

Apps & Dashboards

  • OpenCPN for navigation & charts
  • Grafana + InfluxDB for telemetry
  • Node-RED for automation workflows
  • Optional: Home Assistant integration

AI Touchpoints

  • Automated log triage and summarization
  • Pre-departure safety checklists
  • Anomaly detection (on-device or edge)
  • Natural language queries and alerts

Hardware Profile

  • Raspberry Pi 4/5 (8GB recommended)
  • CAN/NMEA2000 interface (MCP2515/USB2CAN)
  • LTE/WiFi router (e.g., Teltonika)
  • GNSS module, IMU, environmental sensors

Bill of Materials (Estimated)

  • Raspberry Pi 4/5 (8GB) + 32–64GB SD card
  • USB2CAN adapter or MCP2515 HAT
  • NMEA2000 cabling & connectors
  • LTE/WiFi router (e.g., Teltonika RUT240/RUT950)
  • GNSS module (GPS antenna/puck)
  • IMU sensor (pitch/roll/yaw detection)
  • 12V→5V DC converter + fuses & wiring
  • Enclosure (marine-rated, IP65+)

Total hardware cost: approx. $400–800 depending on components selected

Risks & Mitigations

PGN Quirks

Validate NMEA messages with canboat tools and device-specific testing

Power Quality

Brownout protection, surge suppression, proper fusing & wire gauge

RF Noise

Proper cable shielding, grounding, ferrite cores on data lines
